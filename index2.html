Glossary

Essential terminology for understanding blockchain gaming and asset trading.

The blockchain enables secure transactions through smart contracts. Users can provide liquidity to earn fees.

Blockchain Basics

Blockchain

A decentralized, digital ledger that records transactions across a network of computers. It's the technology that powers cryptocurrencies and digital assets.

Smart Contract

Self-executing programs stored on a blockchain that automatically process transactions when predetermined conditions are met.

DeFi (Decentralized Finance)

A financial system built on blockchain technology that operates without traditional intermediaries like banks. DeFi platforms enable lending, borrowing, trading, and earning interest through smart contracts and decentralized protocols.

SPL Token

Solana Program Library Token - the standard for creating digital assets on the Solana blockchain, similar to ERC-20 tokens on Ethereum.

Consensus Mechanism

The protocol that determines how network participants agree on the validity of transactions. Solana uses Proof of History (PoH) and Proof of Stake (PoS).

Crypto Wallet

A digital tool that stores private keys and allows users to interact with blockchain networks, send/receive tokens, and manage digital assets.

Asset Trading

Liquidity Pool

A collection of funds locked in a smart contract that enables trading between different assets. Users can contribute to these pools to earn fees.

Slippage

The difference between expected and actual trade prices, typically occurring in larger transactions or low-liquidity markets.

Automated Market Maker (AMM)

A decentralized exchange protocol that uses mathematical formulas to price assets and enable automated trading without traditional order books.

Impermanent Loss

The temporary loss of funds liquidity providers might experience when the price of deposited assets changes compared to when they were deposited.

Asset Wrapping

Wrapped Asset

A tokenized version of another asset, maintaining the same value while adding blockchain functionality and cross-platform compatibility.

Backing Ratio

The relationship between wrapped tokens and their underlying assets, typically 1:1 to ensure proper value representation.

Asset Bridge

A protocol that enables assets to be transferred between different platforms or networks while maintaining their value.

Asset Custody

The secure storage and management of digital assets, often involving multi-signature security and automated verification systems.

Trading Mechanics

Yield Farming

The practice of staking or lending crypto assets to generate returns in the form of additional cryptocurrency.

Price Impact

The effect that a trade has on the market price of an asset, typically larger for bigger trades or smaller liquidity pools.

Flash Loan

A type of uncollateralized loan where assets are borrowed and repaid within the same transaction, often used in arbitrage.

Arbitrage

The practice of taking advantage of price differences between markets to generate profit with minimal risk.
